## The thesis
the company has publicly committed to doubling from ₹10,000+ crore (FY25, **VERIFIED**) to ₹20,000 crore by its centenary year 2029 (**VERIFIED**). That doubling will be won or lost in decisions made weekly across 5,000+ distributors, ~15 lakh directly-served outlets and 15+ own manufacturing sites (**VERIFIED**). AI's job here is not novelty — it is decision quality at that scale: forecast what the trade will pull, prove which rupee of trade spend works, and stop reading documents by hand. Management already names AI and automation as a growth enabler (**VERIFIED**).

## The three moves that matter first (all three exist as working prototypes)
1. **Demand → Supply: a forecasting + S&OP what-if cockpit** that adds causal promo-ROI and constrained scenario simulation on top of the announced o9/PwC planning rollout (**VERIFIED**) — complement, not competitor. *Prototype: 297,610-row synthetic spine, reconciliation-gated what-ifs, 3.13× launch pipeline-fill reproduced.*
2. **Knowledge → Action: a role-scoped enterprise copilot** — cited answers from policies/SOPs, governed self-serve SQL, margins visible only to roles that may see them. *Prototype: 24-doc governed corpus, 25-question eval incl. role-leak and prompt-injection tests.*
3. **Document → Decision: straight-through processing** for invoices, trade claims, then contracts — rules decide, calibrated confidence gates, every override learned from. *Prototype: 40 doc sets, 13 planted discrepancies, 40/40 ground-truth labels re-derived from written rules.*

Five further scored opportunities (inventory optimisation, content studio, WhatsApp trade bot, shelf-vision, vision QC) complete a top-8 portfolio selected from 30 use cases scored across 8 functions.

## The economics, stated like an analyst (every figure is a labelled ESTIMATE with stated basis)
- 12-month programme spend: **₹4.1–6.2 cr** (three phases, gate-controlled — spend stops if gates fail)
- Modelled net recurring benefit: **₹9.7 cr/yr conservative · ₹17.9 base · ₹26.2 aggressive** (aggregator sums the 8 audited case models; low/mid/high of every stated range)
- One-time working-capital release: **₹30–67 cr** (forecast-led inventory reduction, Cases 1+4)
- Month-12 pass/fail gate: **≥₹6 cr gross measured benefit** — below the conservative sum of the four Phase-0/1 cases (₹6.6 cr), i.e., the plan is designed to clear its own bar
- Honesty note: people-time savings only monetise if managers redeploy the time; headcount and volume assumptions carry ±50% sensitivity and are replaced with actuals in a week-1 data audit.

## Governance before scale
A standing AI Council; DPDP-aligned data classification and consent handling; dual-layer access control (retrieval tags + SQL column policy — already demonstrated in the copilot prototype); three human-in-the-loop tiers with promotion only on measured error rates; model cards, drift monitors, immutable decision logs, and an annual independent audit of any system that acts autonomously. Nothing automates until it has beaten the incumbent process in a measured shadow run.

## The ask (three decisions)
1. Name an executive sponsor and convene the AI Council (month 0).
2. Grant a two-week data audit across DMS/ERP extracts to replace estimates with actuals and re-score the portfolio.
3. Approve Phase 0: three shadow-mode pilots (copilot, document automation, demand cockpit) at **₹0.9–1.4 cr**, judged only against pre-agreed exit gates.
